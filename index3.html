Favorites

Tip #1 (Measuring Flour): Did you know there is a proper way to measure flour? If you just scoop your measuring cup into your flour bag, you may not be getting enough! Instead, you should spoon flour into the measuring cup and then shake the measuring cup to level. This method may be a little more tedious, but it will ensure you get the proper amount.

Tip #2 (Follow the Buttery Directions): One thing I have definitely learned is to listen to the consistency directions when it comes to butter. This means that softened is not the same thing as melted - I speak from personal experience that it will make a big difference! I usually microwave my butter to get it to the right consistency, so microwave in small amounts of time so you don't accidentally melt your butter if you're not supposed to.

Tip #3 (Chilling the Dough): Speaking of things that make a surprisingly big difference, if the recipe calls for chilling the dough, make sure you stick that dough in the fridge! If you want soft cookies, this chilling step is key.

Tip #4 (Read the Whole Recipe): I know, I know, this seems obvious, but make sure you read the entire recipe before you begin. Again, speaking from personal experience, nothing is more frustrating than starting the recipe only to find out you don't have the right ingredients or enough time to finish.
